# What is Off-Gassing

**Off-gassing is the release of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and other chemicals into the air from a new product, most commonly observed with foam mattresses, furniture, and other synthetic household items.**

When products like memory foam or polyurethane foam mattresses are manufactured, they undergo chemical processes that can leave trace amounts of unreacted compounds within the material. These compounds, which can include formaldehyde, benzene, and various other VOCs, are not permanently trapped. Once the product is unpackaged, these chemicals slowly evaporate or "off-gas" into the surrounding air at room temperature. This process is most noticeable with new items and typically diminishes significantly after a few hours to a few weeks, though very low-level emissions can continue for much longer.

The most immediate sign of off-gassing is a distinct, often chemical or "new" smell. For example, when you unroll a new bed-in-a-box memory foam mattress, the initial odor you detect is a direct result of this process. While the smell is often the most noticeable aspect, the primary concern for consumers revolves around the nature of the chemicals being released and potential health effects, which can range from short-term irritation (like headaches, dizziness, or eye/nose/throat irritation) to long-term concerns with prolonged, high-level exposure.

**Why it matters for mattress shoppers:** Understanding off-gassing is crucial for making an informed purchase, particularly for those with chemical sensitivities, allergies, asthma, or for families with young children. To minimize exposure, a key strategy is to allow a new mattress to air out in a well-ventilated room for several days before sleeping on it. Many manufacturers now address this concern by producing mattresses certified to low-emission standards (like CertiPUR-US® for foam or OEKO-TEX® for textiles), which limit the amount and type of VOCs allowed. Checking for these certifications is a practical step toward reducing potential off-gassing.
