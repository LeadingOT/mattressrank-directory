# What is Firmness Scale

**The Firmness Scale is a standardized rating system, typically ranging from 1 to 10, used to describe the perceived softness or hardness of a mattress when initially laid upon.**

This scale provides a common language for manufacturers, retailers, and consumers to communicate about a mattress's feel. A rating of 1 represents the softest possible feel (like sinking into a fluffy pillow), while a 10 represents the firmest feel (similar to lying on a solid board). Most mattresses fall within the 3 to 8 range, often categorized as Soft (3-4), Medium Soft (5), Medium (6), Medium Firm (7), and Firm (8). It's crucial to understand that firmness is distinct from support; firmness is the initial comfort layer, while support refers to the underlying structure that keeps your spine aligned.

The scale is inherently subjective, as an individual's perception of firmness is influenced by body weight, shape, and personal preference. A 160-pound person may rate a mattress as a 6 (Medium), while a 230-pound person may compress the comfort layers more and rate the same mattress as a 4 (Soft). Therefore, the scale is a helpful starting point, not an absolute truth. For example, a "Plush" model from one brand might be a 4 on the scale, while another brand's "Plush" could be a 6.

Understanding the firmness scale matters because choosing the wrong firmness is a leading cause of mattress returns and discomfort. It serves as a critical filtering tool. Side sleepers generally require a softer surface (4-6) to cushion shoulders and hips, while stomach sleepers typically need a firmer surface (7-8) to prevent the spine from sagging. Back sleepers often find a medium-firm feel (6-7) optimal. Use cases include comparing different models online, communicating your preference to a salesperson, or narrowing down options based on your primary sleep position and body type.
