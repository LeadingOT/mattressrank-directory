# What is Motion Isolation

**Motion isolation is a mattress's ability to absorb and localize movement, preventing it from transferring across the sleep surface.**

Motion isolation is a physical property determined by a mattress's construction and materials. When pressure is applied to one area (e.g., a person turning over), a mattress with good motion isolation will contain that energy. The materials, such as memory foam, latex, or individually wrapped coils, work to dampen the vibrations and shockwaves of movement so they are not transmitted to other parts of the bed. In contrast, mattresses with poor motion isolation, often traditional interconnected innersprings or very bouncy materials, act more like a trampoline or a drumhead, allowing movement to ripple across the entire surface.

This characteristic is measured and observed in product testing, where weights are dropped on one side of a mattress while sensors measure the disturbance on the other side. The goal is minimal detectable movement transfer. It is distinct from overall support or firmness; a mattress can be very firm and still have excellent motion isolation if it uses the right materials to absorb kinetic energy.

Motion isolation is critically important for couples and co-sleepers. If one partner is a restless sleeper, gets up frequently during the night, or has a different sleep schedule, strong motion isolation ensures the other partner remains undisturbed. This directly contributes to improved sleep quality and continuity. It is also a valuable feature for those who share a bed with pets or children. For solo sleepers, motion isolation is generally less of a priority unless they are particularly sensitive to their own movements.
