# What is Mattress Warranty

**A mattress warranty is a manufacturer's guarantee to repair or replace a mattress if it develops specific defects in materials or workmanship within a defined period.**

A mattress warranty is a contractual agreement, not a promise of comfort or a guarantee that the mattress will last the full warranty length. It specifically covers flaws that arise from the manufacturing process, such as broken coils, significant foam splitting or cracking, or issues with the foundation that cause visible sagging beyond a certain depth (often 1.5 inches). Importantly, it does not cover normal wear and tear, changes in comfort preference, or sagging that results from an improper foundation.

The structure of these warranties is critical. Most are "prorated," meaning the manufacturer's coverage decreases over time. For example, a 10-year warranty might offer full replacement for defects in the first two years (the "full warranty" period), but in years 3-10, you may be responsible for a percentage of the current retail price, plus shipping and handling fees. Warranties also come with a long list of stipulations, such as requiring a specific, supportive bed frame or foundation, and mandating the use of a mattress protector to avoid stains that would void coverage. Failure to meet these requirements typically invalidates the guarantee.

Understanding your mattress warranty matters because it sets realistic expectations for protection and outlines your responsibilities as an owner. Its primary use case is providing a remedy for a structural failure, like a collapsed edge or a broken innerspring unit. For instance, if a memory foam mattress develops a permanent body impression deeper than the stated sagging threshold (e.g., 1.5 inches) within the non-prorated period, the warranty should cover a repair or replacement. Always register your warranty, keep your receipt, and familiarize yourself with the claim process and exclusions to ensure you can leverage the coverage if a legitimate defect occurs.
