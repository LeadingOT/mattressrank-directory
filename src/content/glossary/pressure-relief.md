# What is Pressure Relief

**Pressure relief is the ability of a material or system to redistribute body weight away from bony prominences to reduce localized stress and improve comfort.**

In the context of mattresses and sleep products, pressure relief refers to the cushioning effect that prevents excessive force from building up on areas like the shoulders, hips, and heels. When you lie down, your body weight is not distributed evenly; these bony points bear a disproportionate amount of pressure. A surface with poor pressure relief will resist conforming, creating high-pressure zones that can restrict blood flow, cause discomfort, and lead to tossing and turning. Effective pressure relief materials, such as memory foam, latex, or advanced polyfoams, softly contour to the body's shape, spreading the load over a wider area.

The primary goal is to lower "interface pressure," the measurable force per unit area between the body and the surface. This is distinct from general support, which pertains to spinal alignment. A mattress can be very firm and supportive yet offer poor pressure relief, resulting in discomfort at key contact points. Conversely, a surface can be excellent at pressure relief but lack the underlying support to keep the spine neutral. The ideal sleep system balances both.

**Why it matters:** Effective pressure relief is crucial for reducing aches and pains, minimizing sleep disturbances, and enhancing overall sleep quality. It is particularly important for side sleepers, whose hips and shoulders drive deep into the mattress, and for individuals with conditions like arthritis, fibromyalgia, or limited mobility. Good pressure relief also helps prevent pressure ulcers (bedsores) for those who are bedridden.

**Related Terms:** *Contouring*, *Pressure Mapping*, *Interface Pressure*, *Pressure Ulcer Prevention*, *Conformity*.
