# What is Edge Support

**Edge support refers to the structural reinforcement built into the perimeter of a mattress to prevent sagging and maintain a consistent sleeping surface when sitting or lying near the edge.**

A mattress's edge support is primarily provided by a reinforced border, typically constructed using high-density foam encasement, firmer foam rails, or specialized innerspring systems like pocketed coils with a stiffer perimeter. This reinforcement combats the natural tendency for the softer comfort layers in the center of the mattress to compress significantly at the edges. Without it, the perimeter can feel unstable and collapse when weight is applied, making the usable sleeping area smaller and creating a "roll-off" sensation.

The quality of edge support directly impacts the functionality and longevity of a mattress. A mattress with strong edge support offers a fully usable surface, allowing you to sit comfortably on the side to put on shoes or sleep near the edge without feeling like you might slide off. This is especially critical for couples who need to utilize the entire mattress width, as it prevents the feeling of being funneled toward the middle. Over time, robust edge support also helps prevent premature sagging and body impressions along the perimeter, which are common failure points that can shorten a mattress's lifespan.

**Why it matters / Use cases:**
Edge support is crucial for maximizing usable sleep space, safety, and ease of use. It is particularly important for:
*   **Couples and combination sleepers:** They can sleep near the edge without instability, effectively gaining more personal space.
*   **Individuals with mobility issues:** A firm, supportive edge makes it safer and easier to get in and out of bed.
*   **Those who use the bed as seating:** Sitting on the edge for morning routines will be comfortable and secure.
*   **Heavier individuals:** They place more stress on the mattress perimeter and require reinforced edges to maintain structure and prevent early sagging.
