# What is Cooling Technology

**Cooling technology refers to the materials, systems, and engineering designs integrated into a mattress or its accessories to actively dissipate body heat and maintain a comfortable, temperature-neutral sleep surface.**

At its core, cooling technology works to counteract the body's natural thermoregulation process during sleep, where heat is released and can become trapped in traditional mattress materials like memory foam. This is achieved through several key methods. **Conductive cooling** uses materials with high thermal conductivity, such as gel-infused foams, graphite, or copper fibers, to pull heat away from the body. **Phase-change materials (PCMs)** are substances engineered to absorb and store excess heat as they melt (change phase) at a specific temperature, releasing it back when the environment cools. **Breathability and airflow** are enhanced through open-cell foam structures, ventilated panels, or mattress covers made from moisture-wicking fabrics (e.g., Tencel, Outlast) that promote air circulation and evaporative cooling.

These technologies are not mutually exclusive and are often layered for a synergistic effect. For instance, a mattress may combine a breathable, moisture-wicking cover with a gel-infused foam comfort layer and a coil support system that allows for significant internal airflow. Some advanced systems even incorporate **active cooling**, using powered devices like bed fans or water-based pads that circulate temperature-controlled fluid through a network of tubes.

This matters because sleep quality is profoundly impacted by temperature. A cooler sleep environment helps facilitate the drop in core body temperature necessary to initiate and maintain deep sleep. For individuals who are naturally hot sleepers, experience night sweats, or live in warm climates, effective cooling technology can be the critical factor in achieving restful sleep. Practical use cases extend beyond the mattress core to include cooling mattress toppers, pillows with gel or PCM layers, and breathable, temperature-regulating sheets and protectors.

**Related terms:** Breathability, Phase-Change Material (PCM), Thermal Conductivity, Moisture-Wicking, Gel-Infused Memory Foam, Hybrid Mattress.
