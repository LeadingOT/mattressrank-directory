# What is Sleep Trial

**A sleep trial is a risk-free evaluation period offered by mattress companies that allows customers to test a mattress at home with the option to return it for a full refund if unsatisfied.**

Unlike a simple return policy, a sleep trial is a dedicated, extended period—typically ranging from 30 to 365 nights—designed to let your body fully adjust to a new sleep surface. The process usually involves a mandatory break-in period (often 30 days) where returns are not permitted, acknowledging that it takes several weeks to acclimate to a new mattress's support and feel. If, after this initial period, you decide the mattress isn't right, you contact the company to initiate a return. Most brands then arrange for a free pickup and either donate or recycle the returned mattress, issuing a full refund to your original payment method.

This model matters because it directly addresses the primary challenge of buying a mattress online: the inability to test it in person for more than a few minutes in a store. A sleep trial shifts the "showroom" to your bedroom, allowing you to assess comfort, support, and temperature regulation over many nights and across different seasons. It significantly reduces purchase anxiety and financial risk, empowering consumers to make confident decisions based on real, long-term experience rather than brief impressions.

Key considerations when evaluating a sleep trial include its length, any hidden costs (like return shipping fees, though most are free), the condition required for a return, and whether a warranty remains separate. It's a cornerstone of the direct-to-consumer mattress industry and is often paired with policies like free shipping and returns. For the consumer, the primary use case is to safely trial a major purchase, while for companies, it serves as a powerful marketing tool that builds trust and reduces barriers to online sales.
