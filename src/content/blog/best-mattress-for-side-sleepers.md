# Best Mattress for Side Sleepers 2026: Top Picks for Shoulder & Hip Pain Relief

If you sleep on your side, you're in the majority — roughly 60-70% of people favor side sleeping. But it comes with a specific requirement: your mattress needs to cushion pressure points while keeping your spine straight. The wrong mattress means shoulder pain, hip discomfort, and numb arms by morning.

## Why Side Sleepers Need a Special Mattress

When you sleep on your side, your shoulders and hips are the main pressure points. They bear most of your body weight against a relatively small surface area. A mattress that's too firm won't let your shoulders and hips sink in enough, creating painful pressure points. One that's too soft lets your spine curve out of alignment.

**The ideal side-sleeper mattress does three things:**
1. **Cushions shoulders and hips** (softer top layers)
2. **Keeps your spine aligned** (supportive core)
3. **Distributes weight evenly** (prevents pressure build-up)

## What Firmness Is Best for Side Sleepers?

Side sleepers generally need **medium-soft to medium (3-6 out of 10)** firmness.

| Body Weight | Ideal Firmness | Why |
|-------------|---------------|-----|
| **Under 130 lbs** | Soft to Medium-Soft (3-4/10) | Less weight = need more give to sink in |
| **130-230 lbs** | Medium (5-6/10) | Balanced cushioning without bottoming out |
| **Over 230 lbs** | Medium-Firm (6-7/10) | More weight compresses foam; need firmer feel |

## Side Sleepers Should AVOID These Mattress Types

❌ **Extra-firm mattresses (8-10/10):** Won't let your shoulders and hips sink in. Hello, numb arms.

❌ **Thin comfort layers (<2 inches):** Not enough material to cushion pressure points.

❌ **Traditional firm innerspring with no pillow top:** The springs push back against your pressure points.

❌ **Cheap, low-density foam:** Sag quickly; you'll be sleeping on the support layer within a year.

---

## How to Test If a Mattress Works for Side Sleeping

1. **Lie on your side** with your normal pillow
2. **Check shoulder comfort:** Does your shoulder feel compressed or cradled?
3. **Check hip sinkage:** Your hip should sink in enough that your spine stays straight
4. **Check spinal alignment:** Have someone look — your spine from neck to tailbone should form a straight line
5. **Check for numbness:** Stay in position 5-10 minutes. Any tingling or numbness?
6. **Try switching sides:** Does the mattress respond quickly enough?

---

## Side Sleeper Pillow Matters Too!

Your pillow is just as important as your mattress. Side sleepers need a higher-loft pillow (4-6 inches) to fill the gap between your shoulder and head, keeping your neck aligned with your spine. Look for:

- **Contoured pillows** with a higher side for side sleeping
- **Adjustable fill** pillows (shredded memory foam or latex)
- **Firm enough** to support your head without collapsing
